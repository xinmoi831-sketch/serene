Medical Disclaimer

Last updated: May 2026

🏥 Serene is NOT a medical device and is NOT a replacement for professional mental health care. Please read this carefully.

Serene is a Support Tool Only

Serene provides emotional support, general wellness information, and a safe space to express your feelings. It is powered by artificial intelligence and is not staffed by licensed healthcare professionals.

What Serene Cannot Do

  • Diagnose any mental health condition
  • Prescribe or recommend specific medications
  • Provide clinical therapy or psychiatric treatment
  • Replace consultation with a licensed professional
  • Guarantee the accuracy of health information provided

When to Seek Professional Help

Please contact a licensed mental health professional if you experience:

  • Persistent depression or anxiety lasting more than two weeks
  • Thoughts of suicide or self-harm
  • Hallucinations or loss of touch with reality
  • Severe mood swings or behavioral changes
  • Substance dependency or addiction

Emergency Situations

🚨 If you or someone you know is in immediate danger, call local emergency services immediately. In Zambia, call 999, 991, 993, or 112 depending on the emergency. Do not rely on Serene in an emergency.

Information Accuracy

While Serene strives to provide helpful and accurate wellness information, AI-generated content may contain errors or outdated information. Always verify medical information with a qualified healthcare provider.

No Doctor-Patient Relationship

Using Serene does not create a doctor-patient or therapist-client relationship. The conversations you have with Serene are for informational and emotional support purposes only.

Crisis Response Policy

Last updated: May 2026

🚨 If you are in immediate danger right now, please call local emergency services immediately. You can also use the Hotlines page for local support numbers. Serene cannot physically intervene or guarantee your safety.

Support Resources

Support button: use the in-app support button when it appears to book a session or open Hotlines.

Hotlines: open the Hotlines page for local support numbers.

Local emergency services: if you are in immediate danger, call 999, 991, 993, 112, or your nearest emergency service.

How Serene Handles Crisis Situations

  • Serene automatically detects crisis language in conversations
  • When detected, Serene switches to a calm, short-response safety mode
  • Serene points you toward the support button, Hotlines page, and local emergency services in these situations
  • Serene encourages you to contact a real person

Limitations of AI in Crisis

Serene is an AI and has important limitations in crisis situations:

  • It cannot call emergency services on your behalf
  • It cannot physically be present with you
  • It cannot guarantee response times
  • It may not always perfectly detect crisis language

Our Commitment

We are committed to building Serene in a way that prioritizes your safety above everything else. If you have feedback about how Serene handled a crisis situation, please contact us so we can improve.

What to Do Right Now If You Are in Crisis

  • Tell someone you trust what you are feeling
  • Open the Hotlines page for local support numbers
  • Go to your nearest hospital emergency department
  • Call emergency services if you are in immediate danger
